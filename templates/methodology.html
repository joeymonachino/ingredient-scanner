Methodology

How Ingredient Scanner decides what to flag.

The app uses a layered method: curated ingredient policy records when available, heuristic fallback when an ingredient looks real but is not yet in the catalog, and an explicit unknown state when confidence is too low.

1. Policy Catalog

The strongest classification path

When an ingredient matches a curated policy record, the app can use fields like processing level, functional role, whole-food alignment, organic alignment, controversy level, traits, note text, and source links.

2. Heuristic Fallback

Used carefully

If an ingredient is not yet in the catalog but clearly looks like a real additive or ingredient name, the app can still estimate a result. Those cases are explicitly labeled heuristic-only so users know the result is weaker.

3. Unknown Input

No fake certainty

If the text does not match a policy record and does not look like a plausible ingredient, the app returns unknown and tells the user it could not confidently identify the input.

Product Verdict Logic

How label-level results are built

  • If any ingredient is avoid, the overall product currently lands in avoid.
  • If there are no avoids but one or more caution ingredients, the product lands in caution.
  • If the ingredients are mostly minimally processed and policy-aligned, the product can land in okay.
  • If the label cannot be interpreted confidently, it can fall into needs context.

Limitations

What the app does not claim

Ingredient Scanner does not claim to settle every safety debate, medical question, or nutrition question. It is a cleaner-label policy tool built around a stricter ingredient-conscious standard, not a substitute for personal research or medical guidance.

The methodology will keep improving as the ingredient catalog expands and more policy records are supported with better sourcing.
